Project Overview

The purpose of this project is to analyze road accident data and generate insights that can be used to improve road safety measures. The data focuses on key factors such as accident severity, vehicle type, road surface, light conditions, and whether the accident occurred in urban or rural areas.

Key Features:

Accident Severity Analysis: Breaks down the number of casualties into categories like Fatal, Serious, and Slight.

Vehicle Type Distribution: Analyzes the types of vehicles involved in accidents.

Road Surface and Type: Understands how different road conditions (dry, wet, snow/ice) impact accidents.

Urban vs. Rural: Distinguishes accident trends based on whether the accident occurred in an urban or rural area.

Monthly Trends: Displays accident data by month to help identify seasonal patterns and spikes in accident numbers.

Light Conditions: Examines the impact of daylight and darkness on accident rates.

Data Structure

The data in this project is structured into several categories, with each category providing insights into different aspects of the road accidents:

Primary KPI: Total number of casualties, broken down by severity (Fatal, Serious, Slight).

Secondary KPI: Number of casualties by vehicle type (Cars, Vans, Buses, Bikes, Others).

Monthly Trend: Shows the number of casualties by month for different years (2021, 2022).

Road Surface: Analyzes casualties based on the type of road surface (Slip road, Roundabout, Single carriageway, etc.).

Urban / Rural: Categorizes the data into Urban and Rural accident areas to identify location-based trends.

Light Conditions: Provides insights on accidents based on light conditions (Daylight, Dark).

Key Insights for Stakeholders

Accident Severity Distribution

Insight: A large proportion of casualties are classified as "Slight" (over 80%), while a smaller portion involves "Serious" (14%) and "Fatal" (1-2%) accidents.

Implication: Most accidents result in minor injuries, but the severity rate for serious and fatal accidents remains a significant concern that requires targeted interventions.

Monthly Trends & Seasonal Patterns

Insight: The number of accidents is higher during specific months (e.g., winter months such as December, January, and February) due to adverse weather conditions like snow, ice, and rain.

Implication: Weather conditions play a significant role in increasing accident severity, especially in the colder months. Thereâ€™s a clear seasonal trend, with more accidents occurring in winter, potentially due to slippery road surfaces.

High-Risk Vehicle Types

Insight: The data shows that Cars account for approximately 80% of all casualties, followed by Vans and Bikes. Buses contribute a smaller percentage of accidents but can still cause significant harm when involved.

Implication: Car-related accidents are a primary concern, especially in urban areas, requiring targeted measures for reducing car accidents, particularly in densely populated areas or high-traffic zones.

Impact of Road Surface on Accidents

Insight: Accidents on wet and snow/ice surfaces have a much higher severity rate than on dry roads. For example, snow and ice-related accidents account for a disproportionate number of fatalities and serious injuries.

Implication: Road surface conditions significantly influence accident outcomes. Measures like improved road maintenance, better signage for icy conditions, and enhanced infrastructure could mitigate the impact of adverse weather conditions.

Urban vs. Rural Accidents

Insight: Urban areas account for a large proportion of accidents, but rural areas show higher severity (fatalities and serious injuries). In urban areas, accidents are more frequent but tend to result in fewer fatalities due to faster response times and better traffic management systems.

Implication: While accidents in urban areas are more frequent, the higher fatality rate in rural areas suggests that rural areas may lack sufficient safety measures (e.g., emergency response, road conditions, or traffic enforcement).

Light Conditions

Insight: Accidents during dark conditions (night-time) account for a significant portion of serious accidents, despite being fewer in number compared to daylight accidents.

Implication: Poor visibility at night is a contributing factor to severe accidents, especially in areas lacking streetlights or in areas where drivers may not adjust speed appropriately for night-time driving conditions.

Data-Backed Recommendations for Stakeholders

Improving Infrastructure for Adverse Weather

Recommendation: Given the higher accident rates during snowy and wet conditions, investing in better road infrastructure (e.g., de-icing systems, gritted roads) and improving signage for adverse weather conditions is critical. Additionally, better lighting and reflective road markings in high-risk areas would help mitigate accidents during winter months.

Action: Increase budget allocations for winter maintenance and update road infrastructure, especially in regions prone to snow and ice. Consider implementing automatic de-icing systems at major intersections.

Targeted Safety Campaigns for Cars and Urban Areas

Recommendation: Since cars are the most involved vehicle type in accidents, it's essential to run targeted awareness campaigns on safe driving, particularly in urban areas where traffic congestion may contribute to accidents.

Action: Launch educational campaigns about safe driving in urban settings, focusing on speed limits, seatbelt use, and the dangers of distracted driving. Integrate these campaigns into local media, social platforms, and schools.

Enhanced Traffic Management in Urban Areas

Recommendation: Given the frequency of accidents in urban areas, it would be beneficial to invest in smart traffic systems and automated traffic enforcement (e.g., speed cameras, red-light cameras) to reduce congestion and improve driver behavior.

Action: Introduce or upgrade smart traffic systems in high-density urban areas to improve traffic flow and reduce accidents. Implement stricter enforcement of speed limits and red-light running.

Increased Safety Measures for Rural Areas

Recommendation: Since rural areas exhibit a higher rate of fatalities, improving road safety infrastructure (e.g., wider lanes, better signage, lighting) and enhancing emergency response times should be prioritized.

Action: Improve road signs, lighting, and lane markings on rural roads, especially in accident-prone areas. Consider establishing more frequent police patrols or response units in these regions to lower the fatality rates.

Implementing Stricter Night Driving Regulations

Recommendation: Since night-time accidents are more likely to result in fatalities or serious injuries, improving visibility on roads and enforcing speed regulations at night is essential. Consider implementing lighting improvements in poorly lit areas and ensure that vehicles have properly functioning headlights.

Action: Install more streetlights on high-risk roads, particularly in rural or low-light areas. Encourage regular vehicle inspections to ensure headlights and tail lights are working correctly, especially for vehicles that frequently operate at night.

Develop a Road Surface Maintenance Program

Recommendation: The analysis shows that wet and icy roads contribute to a disproportionate number of accidents. A dedicated road surface maintenance program should be established, especially in regions prone to these conditions.

Action: Prioritize road resurfacing and maintenance schedules that consider seasonal changes. Implement real-time weather updates and alerts for road conditions to inform drivers and local authorities.

Introduce Real-Time Accident Data Monitoring

Recommendation: Implement a system for real-time accident data collection and monitoring. This will allow stakeholders to identify accident hotspots dynamically and deploy resources (e.g., emergency services) more effectively.

Action: Develop or adopt smart technologies for collecting and sharing accident data in real-time with traffic management systems to identify high-risk areas and adjust traffic management strategies accordingly.
